This is a 5V-to-3.3V LDO digital power-board KiCad project. Please use the project files, project notes, and `datasheets/` to review whether the LDO input capacitor (CIN) and output capacitor (COUT) are currently positioned close to the LDO VIN / VOUT pins on the PCB, and make the necessary PCB changes.

This task specifically checks the distance on the PCB from CIN / COUT to the LDO VIN / VOUT pins. Check whether CIN is placed close to the LDO VIN pin, whether COUT is placed close to the LDO VOUT pin, and whether the loops from both capacitors to the LDO pins are short and compact. If CIN / COUT are tight against the input or output connector and significantly farther from the LDO VIN / VOUT pins, a typical issue being capacitors near board-edge connectors and more than ~25 mm away from the LDO, causing the LDO high-frequency current loop to greatly exceed the device expectation, move CIN on the PCB next to the LDO VIN pin and COUT next to the LDO VOUT pin, so the input / output current loops are as short, compact, and low-inductance as possible. The power traces from the connectors to CIN / COUT can then be routed separately. Apart from this, do not modify the LDO body, other components, nets, or unrelated layout.
