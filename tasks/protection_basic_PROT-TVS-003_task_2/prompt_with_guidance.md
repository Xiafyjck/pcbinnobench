This is a KiCad project for a CAN bus TVS protection board. A CAN transceiver (such as SN65HVD230/HVD232 types) connects to the differential bus through CANH/CANL, and the TVS is used for clamping protection under abnormal voltage. Please use the project files, project description, and `datasheets/` to review whether the current TVS reverse working voltage (VRWM) is coordinated with the CAN transceiver normal operating voltage, bus fault tolerance, and common-mode boundaries, and complete the necessary schematic or PCB modifications.

This task focuses on both boundaries for a TVS on the CAN bus: "no false conduction/clamping" and "timely action under abnormal conditions", as well as the tradeoff between CAN-specific differential arrays and single-line ESD diode topologies and their impact on the respective CANH/CANL paths. It is recommended to extract the TVS VRWM, VBR, IPP, and junction capacitance from `datasheets/`, compare them against the normal voltage range and bus fault tolerance interval of CANH/CANL in the CAN transceiver manual, and judge whether the existing device exceeds a boundary on either side; if changed to a single-line ESD topology, protection devices need to be placed separately on CANH and CANL, while keeping reference prefixes, network naming, and electrical intent clear and consistent.
