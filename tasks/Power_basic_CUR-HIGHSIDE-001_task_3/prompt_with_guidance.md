This is a high-side current-sense amplifier KiCad project that uses an INA240-class current-sense amplifier to measure the shunt resistor voltage drop on a 24V industrial power bus. Please use the project files, project notes, and `datasheets/` to review whether the two differential sense traces from shunt resistor R1 to the INA240 IN+ / IN- are symmetric on the PCB and kept away from noise sources, and make the necessary PCB changes.

This task specifically checks the physical implementation of the two differential sense traces from the two ends of R1 to INA240 IN+ / IN- (PCB nets `Net-(R1-Pad2)`, `Net-(R1-Pad3)`): whether both are sampled from the R1 pads in a Kelvin manner, whether they are short, paired, symmetric, and routed on the same layer, and whether one trace runs close to a power switching node or primary-side high-current path while the other detours or changes layers. If the two sense traces are asymmetric, with one close to a high-current/switching-noise region and the other routed on a different layer or along a different path, adjust them on the PCB so that they each fan out from the two end pads of R1 in a Kelvin manner, run tightly paired, on the same layer, away from power switching nodes and high-current return paths, and go directly to the INA240 inputs. Apart from this, do not modify other components, nets, or traces unrelated to this differential pair.
